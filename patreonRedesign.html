PROCESS

Project Development Journey

Research & Inspiration

Feb 02, 2026

Every project begins with planning, and the target for this redesign is Patreon.com. As a regular user, some may find the site easy to use, but what about newcomers who aren't yet used to its layout? Looking from an outside perspective, the navigation to be confusing and repetative, with double navigation bars and a cluttered screen that gets in the way. The goal of this redesign is to make the website cleaner, more modern, and reduce the mental strain on everyday users.

Wireframing & Layout Planning

Feb 09, 2026

Having planned out the assignment, my team and I began the process of designing and wireframing. I myself am not new to using figma as it is a staple i have had to use the past few years. After sketching our paper designs, we made use of figma to turn those designs into interactive prototypes for usability testing and user feedback. The focus during this step in the project is to design a layout that will enable our testers to complete their tasks in the most efficient way possible.

Interface Design & Styling

March 12, 2026

In the past month there have been many changes, and realiscally I should go back and break some of those steps down. After creatinf the wireframes and initiating user testing, we were excited to see the design completed the goal of simple navigation. However through the testing we encountered another, perhaps worse issue, users not already familiar with Patreons business model were not aware of what the website was even for, the intent behind the websites creation was unclear. On the path to correct this error, the entire webside up to this point was scrapped, including the wireframe and current prototype. Using what her learned from the past month of study and critique, we designed the website from the ground up, focusing more on making it more light hearted and easier for new users to understand its purpose. The second time, designing was far easier then the first, as my team had developed a pattern and skill working around each other. We already had the findings from previous surveys, so we were able to bake that feedback into the design from the very start, eliminating much of the revision that would normally come with abandoning a prototype.

Final Development & Testing

March 25, 2026

We have reached the final steps of this redesign, and at present I am satisfied with the current result all things considered. We were forced to condense months worth of planning into the current iteration of the website, so theres no doubt we could have made this far better than what is was. As far as goals are concerned, the navigation and visual presentation are far better then they were and mental load on the users has been reduced signifigantly. I will update this reflection at some point, but I am grateful for this experience. I learned the value of repeat testing, as user feedback gave us a clear direction to move in and allowed us to always know the next course of action. I also learned many new design tricks that will surely become relevant in future projects.
